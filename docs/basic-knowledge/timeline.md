Timeline (Simple Overview)

<br>

This page provides a **simple, neutral timeline** of key figures and events connected to the Islamic understanding of prophethood and revelation.<br>
It is written for beginners and avoids debate or polemics.

<br>

> In Islam, **prophets** were sent to guide people to worship one God and live morally responsible lives.<br>
> Muslims honor many of the same prophetic figures known in Judaism and Christianity.

<br><br>

---

Abraham (Ibrahim) — Peace be upon him

<br>

**Approximate period:** Ancient era (exact dates unknown)<br>

In Islam, **Abraham (Ibrahim)** is one of the most important prophets. He is known for:<br>
- Strong belief in **one God**  
- Rejecting idol worship  
- Teaching sincere devotion and trust in God  

<br>

Abraham is closely connected to the origins of monotheistic faith.<br>
Muslims also associate Abraham with:
- The legacy of worship centered on Mecca  
- Lessons about sacrifice, patience, and obedience to God  

<br><br>

---

Moses (Musa) — Peace be upon him

<br>

**Approximate period:** Ancient era (exact dates unknown)<br>

In Islam, **Moses (Musa)** is a major prophet who delivered God’s message to his people.<br>
He is known for:
- Calling people to worship God alone  
- Receiving revelation and guidance  
- Standing against oppression  

<br>

The Qur’an mentions Moses frequently, often highlighting themes such as:
- Justice and moral accountability  
- Patience under hardship  
- Trust in God during difficult trials  

<br><br>

---

Jesus (Isa) — Peace be upon him

<br>

**Approximate period:** 1st century CE<br>

In Islam, **Jesus (Isa)** is honored as:
- A prophet and messenger of God  
- A teacher of faith, compassion, and righteousness  
- A sign of God’s power, including his miraculous birth  

<br>

Muslims respect Jesus deeply and see him as part of the same prophetic mission:<br>
guiding people to worship God and live ethically.

<br><br>

---

Muhammad — Peace be upon him

<br>

**Approximate period:** 570–632 CE<br>

In Islam, **Muhammad** is the final prophet.<br>
Muslims believe he was sent to:
- Restore and clarify pure monotheism  
- Teach the Qur’an and explain its meaning in practice  
- Establish a community centered on worship, justice, mercy, and responsibility  

<br>

Muhammad’s life is important to Muslims because it shows how Islamic teachings were lived in real life—<br>
through worship, character, and community guidance.

<br><br>

Revelation of the Qur’an

<br>

**Approximate period:** 610–632 CE (about 23 years)<br>

Muslims believe the **Qur’an** is the word of God revealed to Prophet Muhammad through the angel Gabriel.

<br>

Key points about the Qur’an:
- Revealed gradually over many years  
- Recited in Arabic (translations help convey meaning)  
- Provides guidance on belief, worship, ethics, and daily life  

<br>

The Qur’an remains the central source of Islamic guidance and is memorized, recited, and studied by Muslims worldwide.

<br><br>

---

Summary

<br>

This timeline reflects a core Islamic idea:<br>

- God sent guidance to humanity through many prophets  
- Abraham, Moses, Jesus, and Muhammad are among the most well-known  
- The Qur’an is viewed by Muslims as the final revelation in that line of guidance  

<br>

*This is a simplified educational overview.<br>
Exact historical dates and details may vary across historical sources and religious traditions.*
